General Information

Pearl Harbor is a harbor on the island of Oahu, Hawaii, west of Honolulu. On December 7 of 1941, it became the site of a surprise military strike by the Imperial Japanese Navy Air Service against the United States naval base just before 8 in the morning. The Japanese referred to this operation as "Operation Hawaii", "Operation Al" and "Opeartion Z" during its plannings. It was because of this attack, the USA decided to defer from its neutrality in WW2 and decided to join The Allies to fight against The Axis power.

The Attack on Pearl Harbour

What happened during the attack?

Pearl Harbour was attacked by nearly 200 Imperial Japanese aircrafts on the first wave. Various airfields at the harbour were attacked. On one of the airfields, of the 126 planes present, 42 were destroyed completely, 41 were damaged and only 43 left fit for use. Only 6 USA aircrafts got to repel the attackers of the first wave.

Although the second wave of attacks was less successful than the first wave, it still inflicted some very serious damage. The battleship USS Pennsylvania was set ablaze by the bombs and 2 destroyers neared it were destroyed to wrecks as well. The destroyer USS Shaw was also split in half by a great explosion.

Why did Japan attack Pearl Harbour?

Japan had been modernising their economy during the 20th century and wanted to build an empire. Since Japan lacked natural resources, therefore having to invade other countries for their resources. When Japan took over French indochina, USA retaliated by freezing Japan's assests as USA is a close ally to France. Having lost 94% of their oil, Japan planned to take the oil by force. They wanted to attack British Malay and the Dutch East indies but it would most certainly provoke the USA. To blunt this response, the decision was for Japan to attack USA's Pearl Harbour, hoping that USA would negotiate for peace.

Who were involved in the attack of Pearl Harbour?

Prime Minister Tojo Hideki decided on the surpirse attack on Pearl Harbour. Admiral Yamamoto Isoroku, the commander in cheif of Japan's combined fleet, planned the attack on Pearl Harbour very carefully. On November 26th, Vice Admiral Nagumo Chuichi led a fleet that had 6 aircraft carries, 2 battleships, 3 cruisers and 11 destroyers 400+ kilometres to Pearl Harbour.

How did the attack happen?

The plan was devised by Admiral Isoroku Yamamoto, the plan was to surprise attack against the USA at Pearl Harbour. This plan was to cripple USA's naval power in the pacific, making it easier for Japan to take the rest of Southeast Asia. Throughout their journey across the Pacific, they made strict radio silence to avoid getting detected, making this a true sneak attack. When they approached the Hawaiian Islands, they launched an aircraft to scout for American naval vessels locations and assess the defenses at Pearl Harbour. At approximately 7.48am, the Japanese launched their surprise attack. They targetted battleships, cruisers, destroyers, aircraft, and military installations around the harbor. This dealt heavy damage on the American Pacific Fleet, destroying many battleships, aircraft and military installations. On the second wave, they targetted remaining ships and to maximise the damage so the USA couldn't retaliate whatsoever.

What was the impact of this attack?

The attack on Pearl Harbor took the lives and damage for the United States, with over 2,400 Americans killed and numerous ships and aircraft destroyed or incapacitated. This attack caused The United States to declare war on Japan, officially entering World War II, joining The Allies (British Empire, French Empire) to fight against The Axis (Nazi Germany, Italy and Imperial Japan).
